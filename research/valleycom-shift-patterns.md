## Schedule Structure

Valleycom operates **10 teams**, each led by a named supervisor. Each team has a fixed set of shift slots for Dispatchers (COII) and Call Receivers (COI). Employees bid for these slots twice a year (March and September schedule changes).

### Shift Time Format

Times are written as `HH-HHMM` (start hour – end time):

| Format | Start | End | Duration |
|--------|-------|-----|----------|
| 04-1400 | 0400 | 1400 | 10 hrs |
| 06-1600 | 0600 | 1600 | 10 hrs |
| 06-1800 | 0600 | 1800 | 12 hrs |
| 08-1800 | 0800 | 1800 | 10 hrs |
| 12-2200 | 1200 | 2200 | 10 hrs |
| 12-2400 | 1200 | 2400 | 12 hrs |
| 14-2400 | 1400 | 2400 | 10 hrs |
| 16-0400 | 1600 | 0400 | 12 hrs |
| 18-0400 | 1800 | 0400 | 10 hrs |
| 22-0800 | 2200 | 0800 | 10 hrs |

**Dispatchers (COII) and Call Receivers (COI): 10-hour shifts.**
**Supervisors: typically 12-hour shifts** (some have 10-hr).

### Day Pattern Codes

Each slot has a day pattern code — consecutive days using single letters:

| Letter | Day |
|--------|-----|
| S | Sunday |
| M | Monday |
| T | Tuesday or Thursday (consecutive, context resolves) |
| W | Wednesday |
| F | Friday |
| S | Saturday (second S in a sequence) |

**3-day patterns** (used by most Dispatch/CR slots and some supervisors):

| Code | Days |
|------|------|
| SMT | Sun–Mon–Tue |
| MTW | Mon–Tue–Wed |
| TWT | Tue–Wed–Thu |
| WTF | Wed–Thu–Fri |
| TFS | Thu–Fri–Sat |
| FSS | Fri–Sat–Sun |
| SSM | Sat–Sun–Mon |

**4-day patterns** (used by most supervisors):

| Code | Days |
|------|------|
| SMTW | Sun–Mon–Tue–Wed |
| WTFS | Wed–Thu–Fri–Sat |
| TFSS | Thu–Fri–Sat–Sun |
| FSSM | Fri–Sat–Sun–Mon |
| TWT | Also used as 3-day for some supervisor slots |

These 7 × 3-day and 4 × 4-day patterns tile across the full week with overlapping coverage — every day of the week is covered by multiple teams.

## Shift Bid Process

### SE UI Flow (from `Schedule Express Shift Bid Instructions.docx`)

From the employee's perspective in SE:

1. **Find the bid:** `Bidding > Bid on Shifts` — shows all bids the employee has been enrolled in (e.g., "Dispatch or Call Receiver March 2026 Shift Bid"). Click bid name to see details.
2. **Wait for turn:** Employee receives a text notification when it's their turn. The notification appears to come from the person who bid immediately before them.
3. **Open the bid:** `Bidding > Bid on Shifts > [shift name]` — once it's their turn, a **"Bid on Shift" link** appears in the bidding details window (not visible before their turn).
4. **Select shift:** A dropdown of all **still-available** shifts appears. Employee chooses one and clicks "bid."
5. **Warning:** Once the dropdown window closes, the selection is locked. If the employee made a mistake, they must contact the on-duty supervisor immediately — once the supervisor approves, the bid advances and the mistake carries forward.
6. **Confirmation:** Employee receives a notification when their bid is approved, and the new shift appears on their schedule.

**Key constraint:** The "Bid on Shift" action is a one-shot, non-reversible operation from the employee side. Only the supervisor can intervene after submission.

### Overview (from email + `Shift Bid Schedule Sept 2025` sheet)

1. Supervisor sends email announcing bid start date/time + Excel snapshot link
2. Employee views snapshot to see available slots before bidding
3. **Bid is executed in Schedule Express** — employee selects desired slot in SE
4. On-duty supervisor receives notification in SE and approves
5. Once approved, **next employee is notified via text** through SE that it's their turn
6. Employee may bid immediately or wait until their scheduled time slot
7. If issues arise, employee contacts on-duty supervisor

### Bid Order

Determined by **seniority in class** — most senior bids first:
- Dispatchers (COII) and Call Receivers (COI) bid as **separate lists**
- Both lists run simultaneously on 30-minute offsets (Dispatchers at :00, CRs at :30)
- Each person gets a **1-hour window** at a specific day/time

### Bid Schedule Format (from `Shift Bid Schedule Sept 2025` sheet)

The bid runs across multiple days with ~13 slots per day per classification:

| Day | Dispatcher slots | CR slots |
|-----|-----------------|---------|
| Sat Jan 10 | 0800–1900 (13 people) | 0830–1930 (13 people) |
| Sun Jan 11 | 0800–1900 (13 people) | 0830–1930 (13 people) |

## Timeshift Implications

### Data Model
- **ShiftTemplate** needs: `start_time`, `end_time`, `days_of_week[]` (array of day-of-week ints)
- **Team** is a grouping concept: 10 teams, each has one Supervisor assignment + N Dispatch + M CR slots
- A **slot** = ShiftTemplate + role (Supervisor/Dispatch/CR) + team — the thing people bid for
- After bid, a slot is linked to a specific employee for the schedule period

### Bid Mechanics
- Bid order is seniority-in-class, tracked separately for COII and COI
- Bid window: 1 hour per person; system notifies via text when it's your turn
- Supervisor approval per selection before next person is unlocked
- Bid runs over ~4-5 days for a full cohort of ~90 employees
- Schedule period: ~6 months (March–August, September–February)

### Slot Coverage Design
- 10 teams × ~9 slots/team = ~90 total slots
- Each slot has a unique (shift_time, days_pattern) — not all combinations are unique (e.g., 08-1800 SMT appears multiple times in Team 3)
- The same shift time + days can exist in multiple teams (separate slots, different employees)

### Day Pattern Storage
Store as a bitmask or array of weekday integers (0=Sun through 6=Sat). Example: `FSS` = [5, 6, 0], `SMTW` = [0, 1, 2, 3].
