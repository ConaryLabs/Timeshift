# Valleycom Vacation Bid Process

**Source:** 2026 BID Vacation email (Mark Elliott, Jan 15 2026) + `2026 BID VACATION.xlsx`
**Bid Period:** March 1, 2026 – February 28, 2027
**Extracted:** February 2026

---

## Key Differences from Shift Bid

| | Shift Bid | Vacation Bid |
|-|-----------|--------------|
| Mechanism | SE (Schedule Express) | **Email to supervisors** |
| Seniority list | Separate COII / COI lists | **Combined all-Valleycom seniority** |
| Rounds | 1 round | **2 rounds per person** |
| Unit | Shift slot (one item) | Hours (multiple date blocks) |
| Supervisor action | Approve in SE → triggers next bidder | Post to Google Calendar |
| Employee SE entry | Automatic after approval | **Employee enters manually in SE afterward** |
| Duration | ~4.5 days | **~16 days (Jan 16–31)** |

---

## Bid Flow (Employee)

1. Supervisor sends email with Excel link (schedule, instructions, email example)
2. Employee finds their **bid number** and **assigned bid time** in the Excel
3. At their assigned time, employee emails `Supervisors@Valleycom.org` with:
   - Name + Bid number
   - Requested dates (day shift starts) and hours for each block
4. Supervisor reviews; calls if discrepancies
5. Supervisor posts the request to **Time Off Calendar** (Google Calendar) with BID# prefix on name
6. **After bid is complete**, employee enters each day individually into SE using leave code **"BID Vacation"**
7. Process repeats for **Round 2**

BID requests take priority over any posted non-BID requests on the calendar.

---

## Two Rounds

Each employee has **two** assigned bid times — one in Round 1, one in Round 2.

### Round 1 (Jan 16–23)
- Bid numbers: **B1–B107+** (one per employee in seniority order)
- 1st Round Bid Allowance ≈ ~70% of annual accrual (see tier table below)
- **Minimum block size: 20 hours (two consecutive workdays)**
- Exception: may bid over allowance in round 1 if it's **one consecutive block** — but if so, cannot bid in round 2

### Round 2 (Jan 24–31)
- Bid numbers: **C1–C107+** (same seniority order)
- 2nd Round Bid Allowance = Total Bid Hours − 1st Round Total Bid (remaining accrual)
- **Minimum block size: 10 hours** (one workday)
- Up to the employee's remaining annual accrual

## Block Size Rules

### March–August bids
- Based on your **shift pattern** — bid entire consecutive workdays on your rotation
- (E.g., if you work FSS, bid Fri+Sat+Sun blocks)

### September–February bids
Consecutive calendar-day blocks map to vacation hours charged as follows:

| Calendar days | Hours charged |
|---------------|---------------|
| 1 | 10 |
| 2 | 20 |
| 3 | 30 |
| 4 | 40 |
| 5–7 | 80 |
| 8 | 50 |
| 9 | 60 |
| 10 | 70 |
| 11–14 | 80 |

> **Note:** This non-linear table is unusual — 8 days charges fewer hours than 5-7 days. Likely derived from the actual working-shift-days within each calendar span given the rotating schedule. Verify against VCCEA/VCSG contract Article on vacation bidding.

## Google Calendar Integration

The supervisors maintain a **Time Off Calendar** (Google Calendar, publicly linked in the email).
- Approved BID requests are posted with format: `B1 - Employee Name`
- Non-bid requests are posted without BID prefix
- BID requests take priority over non-bid on the same date

This is currently external to SE — SE only gets entries **after** the bid, entered manually by the employee.

---

## SE Entry — Not Actually Used

The email instructs employees to enter approved vacation days into SE using leave code **BID Vacation**, but **Valleycom does not actually use SE for vacation tracking in practice**. The Google Calendar is the source of truth for vacation, not SE. SE vacation entries are effectively unused.

Timeshift should own the full vacation bid workflow natively.

---

## Timeshift Implications

### Vacation Bid is Email-Based — Not SE-Native
The current process is manual: email → supervisor posts to Google Calendar → employee enters SE.
Timeshift could own this entire flow:

1. System presents bid schedule to each employee at their assigned time window
2. Employee submits bid blocks (dates + hours) within the system
3. Supervisor approves/flags discrepancies
4. System automatically records approved leave entries
5. Calendar view reflects BID vs non-BID requests with priority

### Data Model Additions Needed
- **`vacation_bids`** table: `employee_id`, `bid_year`, `bid_number`, `round` (1 or 2), `bid_window_start`, `status`
- **`vacation_bid_entries`** table: `bid_id`, `start_date`, `end_date`, `hours`
- Bid order: per-year ordered seniority list (different from shift bid — combined all-class seniority)
- Annual accrual hours per employee (drives allowance calculation)

### Seniority
- Vacation bid seniority = **overall Valleycom hire date** (not classification-based)
- Need a `valleycom_seniority_date` or `hire_date` field on users separate from "seniority within class"

### Bid Allowance Rule
- Round 1 allowance is not a simple percentage — it steps by tier. Store as a lookup or compute from accrual tier.
- Round 2 allowance = total annual accrual − round 1 hours actually bid

### Hours Tracking
- `vacation_hours_balance` per employee per year needed (alongside existing `ot_hours` table)
- Vacation accrual rate stored per employee (varies 54–228 hrs/yr based on seniority tier)
