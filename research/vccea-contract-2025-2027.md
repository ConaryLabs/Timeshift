# VCCEA Working Agreement 2025-2027 — Key Rules for Timeshift

**Source:** Valley Communications Center / Valley Communications Center Employees' Association
**Contract Period:** January 1, 2025 – December 31, 2027
**Source document:** PowerDMS, "2025-2027 VCCEA Amended CBA Signed 010126" (searchable version)
**Extracted:** Feb 2026

---

## Job Classifications (Article 1)

VCCEA covers two bargaining unit classifications:

| Code | Full Name | Primary Duty |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| `COI` | Communications Officer I | 911 call receiving |
| `COII` | Communications Officer II | Police, fire, and medical dispatch |

**Supervisors are NOT in VCCEA** — they are in the separate Supervisor Guild.
In SE: A Supervisor = management, B Dispatcher = COII, C Call Receiver = COI.

Employee types:
- **Regular full-time** — 40 hrs/week, full benefits
- **Job Share** — 20 hrs/week, voluntary, max 4 per classification, 6-month increments
- **Medical Part-Time** — 20-39 hrs/week, ADA accommodation only

## Seniority (Article 7)

Three distinct seniority counters per employee:

| # | Type | Basis | Used For |
|---|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | **Overall seniority** | Hire date | Operator number, layoff/recall, reassignment |
| 2 | **Time in bargaining unit** | Total days in VCCEA | Bid vacation selection |
| 3 | **Time in-classification** | Total days as COI or COII | Shift bid selection |

- Seniority accrues while on **paid leave**
- Seniority does **not** accrue on: layoff, unpaid LOA > 30 days (except OJI, pregnancy, military)
- COI promoted to COII: COI time-in-grade does NOT carry to COII
- If promotion fails trial service: all trial service time converts back to COI time-in-grade

---

## Shift Bidding (Section 7.2)

- Occurs **every 6 months** (March and September rotations)
- Basis: **time in-classification seniority**
- Seniority list snapshot: first Sunday in March / first Sunday in September

**Bid process dates:**
- March rotation: bid January 10–15
- September rotation: bid July 23–28

Supervisors bid first, then VCCEA bargaining unit by classification seniority.
Flex shifts: max 2 per classification per cycle, up to 3 consecutive start times.

## Overtime (Article 4.4)

### When OT is triggered
OT process starts when **established minimum staffing levels have not been met**.
Center must complete each step in order until covered. If still uncovered after all steps, Center operates at discretion (minimums, close consoles, management covers).

### OT Rules
- Paid leave counts as hours worked for 24-hour period calculations
- Traded hours count as hours worked for 24-hour period calculations
- OT pay rates:
  - Between regularly scheduled shifts or ≤2 hrs before/after shift: **1.5x base**
  - On holidays: **2x base**
  - Hours 13–14 of consecutive work: **2x base** (2.5x if mandated or holiday)
  - Day off OT (between 2 hrs after Friday and 2 hrs before Monday): **2x base** (2.5x on holidays)

---

### Step 1: Voluntary OT (outside 24 hours)

Posted on daily schedule. **First come, first served.**

- COI OT not signed up within **5 days** → other classifications may work it
- COII OT not signed up within **5 days** → Supervisors may work it
- Time posted with <5 days but >24 hours notice: first come, first served

---

### Step 2: Elective OT (inside 24 hours)

New OT created within 24 hours of the time needing covered, offered in order:

1. **In-classification volunteer** — by overall seniority, employees present in com room
2. **Out-of-classification volunteer** — by overall seniority, VCCEA employees present in com room
3. **Group page** to all com room employees — first qualified VCCEA employee who volunteers
4. **On-duty supervisor** may cover by going into minimums, OR off-duty supervisor volunteer present in com room *(if supervisor doesn't elect to cover minimums here, they cannot again until Mandatory Step 5)*

Employees who elect OT → name moves to back of mandatory list.

---

### Step 3: Mandatory OT (if elective doesn't cover)

Assigned in this order:

1. In-classification employees on **regularly assigned shift** (incl. full 10-hr trades)
2. In-classification employees on **partial trades**
3. Out-of-classification employees on **regularly assigned shift** (incl. full 10-hr trades)
4. Out-of-classification employees on **partial trades**
5. On-duty supervisor may cover by going into minimums
6. **In-classification employees on their day off**
7. Out-of-classification employees on their day off

Employees who are mandated → name moves to back of mandatory list.

**Mandatory OT limits:**
- Max **2 hours** before/after regularly scheduled shift, **1 mandatory assignment** per shift
- Cannot be mandated within **10 hours** of regularly assigned shift (except 2-hr pre/post)
- Max **14 hours** in any 24-hour period, max **12 consecutive hours** (employees may elect up to 14 consecutive by mutual agreement)
- No mandatory OT ≥72 hours prior to employee's regularly assigned shift without consent

---

### OT Card / Tracking System (Section 4.4.2)

- Center maintains an **overtime tracking system by classification**
- Two separate lists:
  1. COI/II employees — for COI overtime
  2. COII employees — for COII overtime
- When employee works OT (>7.5 min holdover): card **moves to back of mandatory list**
- Volunteering, electing, or being mandated for OT: all move name to back of list

> **This is the callout list order system.** It's NOT pure inverse seniority or pure inverse OT hours —
> it's a tracking list where everyone moves to the back after working OT.
> The list order determines who gets called first for mandatory inside-24-hr OT.

---

### OT Bumping (Section 4.5)

Employee A can bump Employee B from **voluntary OT** when:
- B has **less OT** than A (based on tracking system)
- Bump happens **no earlier than one pay period ahead** and **no later than 5 days before**
- A must notify B directly; inside 5 days only by mutual agreement + supervisor notification

**Bumping restrictions:**
- COII/Supervisor **cannot** bump a COI for voluntary COI OT (unless mutual agreement)
- Supervisor **cannot** bump COII for voluntary COII OT (unless mutual agreement)
- Cannot bump in the middle of a 6+ hour block
- Cannot leave the bumped person with ≤2 hours remaining
- **Prohibited on contractual holidays** (unless mutual agreement)

---

### OT Cancellation (Section 4.4.9)

- Voluntary/Elective OT: **cannot be cancelled with <24 hrs notice** (unless mutual agreement)
- Mandatory OT at end of shift: can be cancelled without notice
- Mandatory OT before shift starts: **cannot be cancelled** unless employee agrees
- Mandatory OT ≥4 consecutive hours on day off: **cannot be cancelled** unless employee agrees

---

### Day Off OT (Section 4.4.5–4.4.6)

- Defined as OT worked between 2 hrs after employee's Friday and 2 hrs before their Monday
- Paid at **2x base** (2.5x on holidays)
- Full 10-hr trades = regularly assigned shift for day-off OT purposes
- Partial trade employees: eligible for mandatory OT before/after their trade at 1.5x
- Inside 24 hrs: supervisors may page/call for day-off mandatory (exigent discretion)
- Day off mandatory: scheduled in **4–6 hour increments** (up to 14 hrs by mutual agreement)

---

## Shift Trades (Section 4.6)

- Must be **same job classification** (COI ↔ COI only, COII ↔ COII only)
- Must be submitted in advance **via scheduling platform** for supervisor approval
- Must be completed **on or before the same date of the next consecutive month**
- Employees must honor their trade; if unable, must use **accrued time off**
- **"Double Trade"** (trading a shift already traded once) is **prohibited**
- Permanent trades between employees: allowed if Center approves, no past practice established

**Separation handling:**
- Trade >30 days out when employee separates: Center cancels the trade
- Trade ≤30 days out, separated employee hasn't completed their half: wages deducted from final paycheck
- Trade ≤30 days out, separated employee completed their half, remaining employee hasn't: Center cancels remaining side

**Union Trades (Section 4.7):**
- A one-sided trade (not paid back) for VCCEA officials/representatives
- Requires VCCEA Executive Board approval form before submitting to Center

## Leave / Absences (Article 8)

### Vacation Accrual (Section 8.2)

| Years of Service | Hours/Year |
|-----------------|------------|
| Hire – Year 4 | 108 |
| Years 5–9 | 132 |
| Years 10–14 | 156 |
| Years 15–19 | 180 |
| Years 20–24 | 204 |
| Years 25+ | 228 |

- Accrues per pay period (24 pay periods/year)
- **Max carryover (vacation + holiday combined): 240 hours** — excess cashed out at base rate
- Bid vacation selection uses **time in bargaining unit seniority**
- Bid vacation period: March 1 – last day of February the following year

### Holiday Accrual (Section 8.1)

- **96 hours/year** (FTE × 96)
- 4 hours per pay period per FTE
- 12 observed holidays (listed in contract)
- Combined with vacation for 240-hr max carryover
- Sellback: up to 96 hours **twice annually** (payable first pay dates of June and December)
- Must complete one-on-one COI training to be eligible for sellback

**Guaranteed Holiday Time:** Request >72 hrs in advance for vacant annual vacation calendar slot.
**Non-Guaranteed Holiday Time:** All other holiday time; subject to minimum staffing; confirm within 3 hrs before shift.

### Sick Leave (Section 8.6)

- **8 hours/month** accrual (4 hrs/month for job share/medical PT)
- **Max carryover: 960 hours/year** — excess cashed out at base rate
- Trial service employees: frontloaded **20 hours** on hire (stop accruing until surpassing that timeframe)
- Sick leave payout on retirement: 25% up to $7,500 (after 10 yrs service); 35% up to $14,000 (after 25 yrs)
- Call-in must notify at least **3 hours before shift**, must speak to a supervisor

### Compensatory (Comp) Time (Section 8.3)

- Same approval process as non-bid vacation
- Consider requests ≤30 days before requested date
- Requests within 72 hrs approved if above minimum staffing
- Cannot substitute for bid vacation

### Bereavement (Section 8.7)

- Max **40 hours per calendar year**
- Must notify Center within **3 days** of family member's death
- Immediate family: blood, marriage, domestic partner (with affidavit), legal adoption
- Additional leave may be granted for travel >250 miles each way

### Other Leaves

- **Emergency Leave** — 1 shift or portion, max once per 12 months, deducted from accrued leave
- **Leave of Absence** — up to 6 months unpaid, written application
- **Unpaid Protected Leave** — paid leave used first, may retain up to 80 hrs vacation; 20 of 80 may be sick for maternity/paternity
- **Medical Emergency Leave Sharing** — employees may donate up to 20 hrs/year sick leave if they have ≥100 hrs remaining

## Normal Work Week / Shifts (Section 4.1)

- Standard: **4 × 10-hour days**, followed by 3 consecutive days off
- Alternate: 5 × 8-hour days or non-consecutive days off (for medical accommodation)
- Shift adjustments: minimum **2 weeks notice** (less by mutual agreement)
- Permanent schedule change: mutual agreement required
- Work week max: **40 hours**

---

## Minimum Staffing (Section 4.1)

- Minimum staffing levels established by Center, annotated in ScheduleExpress
- Staffing numbers calculated and **OT posted at least 1 month in advance**
- Once established, minimum staffing levels not changed without legitimate business reason
- When in COI minimums: all employees logged into assigned queue and available (green)

## Key Implications for Timeshift

### OT Callout List
The callout order is **a queue that resets to back on every OT work event** — not a pure seniority or inverse-hours sort. The list is maintained per classification (COI list separate from COII list).

Timeshift needs to track:
- Per-employee, per-classification: their current position in the mandatory OT list
- When OT is worked: move that employee to the back
- Separate handling of voluntary (first come, first served outside 24 hrs) vs elective/mandatory (inside 24 hrs, list-based)

### Classification Rules
- COI and COII are distinct for OT, trades, shift bid, and callout purposes
- Cross-classification OT has specific rules (COI OT can open to COII after 5 days, etc.)
- Supervisors are NOT in VCCEA — their OT is handled differently

### Leave
- Leave balances needed in DB: vacation hours, holiday hours, sick hours, comp hours
- Accrual rates vary by years of service — needs a tenure-based accrual schedule
- Combined vacation+holiday max carryover (240 hrs) complicates simple per-type tracking

### Seniority
- Three separate seniority values must be stored per employee
- Time-in-classification seniority resets on promotion (COI→COII)
- Overall seniority = hire date (simplest)

### Trades
- Trades require same-classification validation before submission
- "Double trade" prohibition must be enforced
- Trades affect OT mandate eligibility (full trade = regularly assigned shift; partial trade = different category)
